1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

In business today, IT or Information and Communications Technology (ICT) is used to securely communicate, collaborate and streamline the work of staff in the pursuit of commerce with clients or customers [1]

1.2 Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully, what do you need to make projects successful? What tools? What skills?

A collaborative endeavour to accomplish a digital project hinges around good project management, leadership/communication skills and technology. [1]

Version Control Systems such as Git & Github, allow workers to share a codebase, divide labour into smaller units to work on separately, avoid collisions with other workers who might try and work on the same file, and track the iterations of the project. This allows code to be experimented on, rolled back to previous versions, or for new versions to be developed and tested, and when ready merged into a new and improved version ready for release. [2]

Kanban, or 'visual cards' software such as Trello can allow teams to collaboratively perform the role of a digital project manager, in a distributed way. Workers can plan and then adjust the plan as new tasks are identified and unforeseen tasks become apparent. Progress can be tracked and the speed of tasks progress be visible by all, helping to identify and defeat bottlenecks in the project before deadlines arrive. [3]

These tools facilitate communication across desks, departments and offices, to enable even the smallest parts of an organisation in being seen and heard for their valuable contributions to overall strategy and workflow.

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

Failure to adequately define and evaluate the projects scope, risks and key assumptions. Scope is the planning of the project, risks are obstructions to the project that may arise and key assumptions are e.g. potentially problematic expectations of things naturally going according to plan.

The projects scope should be structured in such detail that it identifies risky assumptions and bottlenecks in advance. The scope should also define a plan to evaluate the progress of the project. This will flag up emerging difficulties as they arise so effort can be refocused on them.

Failure to plan in detail, track progress and manage risk with formal methods and strategies, as well as managing expectations with the client and maintaining overall communication within the team and between it and the client are what can lead to serious problems with the project, its deadlines and its living up to client specification. [1]

1.5 Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software – how do you select the right kind of technology for the job?

Based upon the clients specfied requirements, i would research what popular tech stacks are often used for projects like these. Research would start with blogs to identify key technologies, then i would see those technologies testimonials and websites for examples of the technology in action, then browse forums and opinion for an overview of developer sentiment regarding its ease of use and performance.

Further review of the selected tech stack would check the solution serves the client needs in the following categories: Size, is the solution of low enough overheads for the project, Licensing, does the client have a budget or not for non-open source technology, Users, will the solution be simplistic enough to use for the target audience, and Growth, will the solution scale appropriately for the clients needs as the company/product grows. [1]

Finally review should ensure in-house compatibility with the skills and ability of the team, are they all senior developers or of a more mixed skill level? A prudent selection might depend on the tech stacks tried-and-true maturity, its ecosystem of online tutorials, experts, helpful forum community, while its outlook for long-term support affects how your team might maintain the project for the client in 5 or 10 years time all things going well. [1]

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

Developers in choosing a tech stack might consider the legal limitations of its licenses before selecting it. Some technologies have licenses which prohibit their use if the product its used to build will be modified, proprietary or for profit.

In the development of customer facing products that store personal data, theres the Data Protection Act (DPA) and General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) to legislate how privacy and data retention policy must be developed and adhered to.

It means the user must be given more choices on how their data is managed, or access on request to their data, or request its deletion. Non-compliance with these consumer rights, or failure to protect personal data can incur large fines for organisations. This effects design choices such as user registration forms, database security steps, reducing personally identifiable details to a minimum, all as part of a methodology of data protection by design and default.

It also means having contingency plans in the event of a data breach to inform customers and the Information Commissioners Office. GDPR compliant design is an important part of any digital project and either an employee who is competent in GDPR or a legal expert should be employed to verify compliance. [1] [2]
